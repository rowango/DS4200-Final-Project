Introduction

Despite numerous public health interventions, the opioid crisis remains a critical challenge for urban centers like Boston. This project analyzes opioid overdose EMS incidents across the city to identify where and when life-saving resources — such as Narcan distribution and mobile harm reduction units — are most needed. By examining location, time, and incident frequency, we aim to provide actionable insights that support the Boston Public Health Commission's population health equity agenda.

Our analysis focuses on trend identification, geographic clustering of hotspots, and correlations with socioeconomic factors at the neighborhood level.

Data

We utilize the Boston EMS Narcotic Related Incidents dataset from the City of Boston Open Data Portal (data.boston.gov). The dataset contains over 2,000 records spanning 2020–2025, with attributes including incident date/time, geographic coordinates, neighborhood, and incident type codes. Additional derived features include month, year, hour of day, and day of week.

Limitations: The dataset captures only EMS-reported incidents, meaning unreported overdoses are not reflected. Socioeconomic data was joined from external census sources and may not perfectly align at the neighborhood level.
